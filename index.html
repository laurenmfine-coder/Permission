How coaching works

A clear path, not a mystery.

Coaching can feel intangible from the outside. Here is what it actually looks like to work with me, from first conversation to the last session and beyond.

01

Discovery call

A free 30-minute conversation. We talk about where you are, what you're navigating, and whether coaching with me is the right fit. No pressure, no pitch.

02

Map the territory

In our first sessions, we surface what's actually at stake. Not the surface question, the underlying one. Values, identity, the story you've been telling yourself, and the one you want to write next.

03

Build the framework

Together we construct the decision architecture: what you actually want, what trade-offs you're willing to make, what permission you've been waiting for. You leave each session with concrete next steps.

04

Move forward

The work continues between sessions, in the choices you make and the conversations you have. By the end, you have not just an answer but a way of thinking that lasts beyond our time together.

About Lauren

I'm the proof
of concept.

I'm a physician first. Allergist, immunologist, still seeing patients one day a week. Then a question about how clinicians learn to think pulled me into medical education and into building tools to teach reasoning. That work led to a TEDx talk, a Substack, and now a coaching practice. If you're a clinician weighing a change, my physician coaching work is built for exactly that.

What ties it all together: each pivot was a decision about what mattered, not what to abandon. That is the work I help others do now. Clarify the inputs. Map the trade-offs. Build the story that lets you choose, and live with the choice.

I'm not a coach who talks about transitions from the sidelines. I've lived every pivot I guide others through, and I haven't given up any of the parts that mattered.
